About OrbitX

Space science made accessible

OrbitX is a modern digital space-exploration platform built to make space science understandable, engaging, and accessible for everyone. From planets and moons to rockets and satellites, OrbitX connects theoretical knowledge with real-world missions and technologies used by global space agencies.

Our goal is to bridge the gap between curiosity and scientific understanding by presenting space data in a structured, visual, and interactive format — whether you are a student, a researcher, or a space enthusiast.

Space science is often scattered across complex research papers, textbooks, and isolated sources. OrbitX brings everything together in one place — verified information, structured learning paths, and visually rich explanations — without overwhelming the learner.

We believe that understanding space should not be limited by access, background, or location. Knowledge of the cosmos belongs to everyone.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is OrbitX?

OrbitX is an educational space-exploration platform that brings together planetary science, space missions, and modern aerospace technology in a simple and interactive way.

Who is OrbitX for?

OrbitX is designed for students, educators, researchers, and anyone curious about space — from beginners to advanced learners.

Is OrbitX free to use?

Yes. OrbitX is completely free and open for learning. No paid subscriptions are required.

Which space agencies and missions are covered?

OrbitX covers major global space agencies such as ISRO, NASA, ESA, JAXA, SpaceX, and other international and private missions.

Is the information on OrbitX reliable?

Yes. All content is curated from verified scientific sources, space agency publications, and publicly available research data.

Can students use OrbitX for projects and research?

Absolutely. OrbitX is ideal for school projects, college assignments, presentations, and introductory research in space science.

Does OrbitX include real images and data from space missions?

Yes. OrbitX uses real planetary imagery, mission visuals, and scientific datasets wherever available.

Will OrbitX add interactive features in the future?

Yes. Upcoming updates include interactive 3D space views, simulations, mission timelines, and advanced learning tools.

How often is the content updated?

Content is updated regularly to include new discoveries, missions, launches, and advancements in space technology.
